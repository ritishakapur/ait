# Reasoning.md

## Rule: How We Think
We think in structures, not streams. Every analysis follows a chain of logic that anyone can follow.

## The Reasoning Chain
1. **Observe** — What do we know? (facts, data, evidence)
2. **Question** — What doesn't make sense? What's missing? What contradicts?
3. **Hypothesize** — What could explain this?
4. **Test** — Does the hypothesis hold against evidence?
5. **Conclude** — What do we actually know now?

## MECE Principle
- **Mutually Exclusive** — No overlap between categories
- **Collectively Exhaustive** — Nothing important is missed
- Apply MECE to everything: stakeholder maps, customer segments, revenue streams, competitor sets

## Avoid These Reasoning Traps
- **Confirmation bias** — Only looking for evidence that supports your preferred answer
- **Anchoring** — Letting the first piece of information dictate your analysis
- **Overgeneralization** — Drawing broad conclusions from narrow data
- **False equivalence** — Treating different things as the same because they share one feature

## What Good Looks Like
- Every conclusion has a visible reasoning chain
- Every category is MECE
- Every trap is acknowledged and avoided
