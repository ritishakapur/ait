# Writing.md

## Rule: How We Write
Clear, specific, and sourced. No fluff. No jargon without definition. No vague claims.

## Writing Standards
1. **One idea per sentence** — If a sentence has two ideas, split it
2. **Specific over general** — "373 lakh transactions" not "many transactions"
3. **Active voice** — "The company launched X" not "X was launched by the company"
4. **Define acronyms on first use** — "CSC e-Governance India Ltd (CSC-SPV)" not just "CSC-SPV"
5. **Use tables for comparisons** — Never describe a comparison in prose when a table works
6. **Flag uncertainty** — "Estimated at X" not "X" when you're estimating

## Tone
- Professional but not academic
- Direct but not blunt
- Analytical but not dry
- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague who doesn't have time for fluff

## Structure
- Headings for sections (H2, H3)
- Tables for structured data
- Bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Code blocks for formats/templates
- Bold for emphasis (sparingly)

## What Good Looks Like
- A CEO can read it in 10 minutes and understand the full analysis
- A new team member can follow the logic without asking questions
- Every claim has a source or is flagged as an estimate
