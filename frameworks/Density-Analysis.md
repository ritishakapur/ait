# Density-Analysis.md

## Purpose
How to analyze where value, activity, or problems are concentrated — and where they're sparse.

## The Density Question
Where is everything concentrated? Where is everything sparse? What happens at the edges?

## What to Measure
- Transaction density (where do most transactions happen?)
- Revenue density (where does most revenue come from?)
- Problem density (where do most complaints/frictions occur?)
- Stakeholder density (where are most stakeholders concentrated?)

## How to Use It
1. Map the density of the metric you're analyzing
2. Identify the hotspots (high density)
3. Identify the cold spots (low density)
4. Ask why the hotspots are hot and the cold spots are cold
5. Ask what would happen if you moved resources from cold to hot (or vice versa)

## Examples
- If 80% of transactions happen in 20% of districts → focus optimization there first
- If 80% of complaints come from one customer segment → that segment needs immediate attention
- If 80% of revenue comes from one product → that product is a risk if it declines

## What Good Looks Like
- Every density analysis has a clear metric
- Hotspots and cold spots are identified
- The "why" is explained
- Actionable recommendations follow
