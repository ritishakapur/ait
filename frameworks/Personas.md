# Personas.md

## Purpose
How to create personas that are useful, not fictional.

## The Persona Framework

### Who They Are
- Name, age, location (realistic, not generic)
- Education, income/economics
- Tools they use daily
- Motivation (money, status, mission, fear, convenience, growth)

### What They Actually Do
- Not their job description — what they actually spend their time on
- What they complain about
- What frustrates them

### What They Need
- What they need from the product/service
- Capability type (Simple/Functional/Judgment/Coordination/Higher order)
- Current solutions (and why they're inadequate)

### What's Implied But Not Said
- Behaviors, workarounds, self-organized solutions
- Things not in any official document

## Persona Rules
- Name them something memorable (not "Segment A")
- Base them on evidence, not imagination
- Include at least 2 gaps per persona (needs not currently served)
- Test each persona against 5 real or plausible people

## What Good Looks Like
- A real person would say "yes, that is me" if shown the persona
- Every need is tagged with a capability type
- Every gap is marked with X
- The persona reveals something the company doesn't officially acknowledge
