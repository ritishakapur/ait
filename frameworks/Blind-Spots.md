# Blind-Spots.md

## Purpose
Systematically identify what we're NOT seeing in our analysis.

## The Blind Spot Checklist

### Data Blind Spots
- [ ] Are there claims based on a single source?
- [ ] Are there numbers that are estimates rather than reported?
- [ ] Are there sources that are older than 18 months?
- [ ] Are there trends stated with only one source?

### Reasoning Blind Spots
- [ ] Are we confirming our pre-existing beliefs?
- [ ] Are we anchoring on the first piece of information?
- [ ] Are we overgeneralizing from narrow data?
- [ ] Are we treating correlation as causation?

### Structural Blind Spots
- [ ] Are we missing a stakeholder layer?
- [ ] Are we missing a revenue stream?
- [ ] Are we missing a competitor?
- [ ] Are we missing a customer segment?
- [ ] Are we missing a technology stack?

### Temporal Blind Spots
- [ ] Are we analyzing the company as it was, not as it is?
- [ ] Are we ignoring recent changes (last 12-18 months)?
- [ ] Are we projecting current trends indefinitely without checking for inflection points?

## How to Use This
Before finalizing any analysis, run through the checklist. Flag every item that applies in the file's Blind Spots section.

## What Good Looks Like
- Every blind spot is documented
- Every blind spot has a confidence level (H/M/L)
- Every high-confidence blind spot has an action item to investigate
