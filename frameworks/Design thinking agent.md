# Design thinking agent.md

## Purpose
How to apply design thinking to business problems — empathy-first, solution-oriented.

## The Design Thinking Process

### 1. Empathize
- Who are we designing for? (from Stakeholders.md)
- What do they actually need? (from Stakeholders.md — Per Layer Detail)
- What frustrates them? (from Customers.md — Pain Points)
- What do they do that's not in any official document? (from Stakeholders.md — Implied But Not Said)

### 2. Define
- What is the real problem? (not the symptom, the root cause)
- Frame it as "How might we...?"
- Make it specific and actionable

### 3. Ideate
- Generate 10+ ideas (don't filter yet)
- Think in buckets: low-hanging fruit, moonshots, incremental improvements
- Combine ideas that overlap

### 4. Prototype
- What's the smallest version we can test?
- What does success look like?
- What's the minimum viable solution?

### 5. Test
- Who will we test with?
- What will we measure?
- What feedback do we need?

## Design Thinking Principles
- Start with the user, not the technology
- Fall in love with the problem, not the solution
- Fail fast, learn faster
- The best solution is the simplest one that works

## What Good Looks Like
- Every solution is grounded in real user needs
- Every prototype is testable
- Every test generates actionable feedback
- The solution addresses the root cause, not the symptom
