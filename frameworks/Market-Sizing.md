# Market-Sizing.md

## Purpose
How to size markets without making up numbers.

## The Bottom-Up Method (Preferred)
1. Start with the smallest unit (one customer, one transaction, one VLE)
2. Multiply by the number of units
3. Cross-check with top-down numbers
4. If they don't match, investigate why

## The Top-Down Method (For Cross-Check)
1. Start with the total market (industry report, government data)
2. Apply the company's market share
3. Cross-check with bottom-up numbers

## The Formula
TAM = Total Addressable Market = everyone who could buy
SAM = Serviceable Addressable Market = everyone you can actually reach
SOM = Serviceable Obtainable Market = everyone you'll actually get in 1-3 years

## Rules
- Every number must have a source or be explicitly labeled as an estimate
- Show your work (the calculation, not just the answer)
- Show both TAM and SAM — they're different numbers
- Flag assumptions clearly
- If your estimate is off by more than 50% from reported data, re-examine your inputs

## What Good Looks Like
- A reader can follow your math step by step
- Every input has a source
- Every assumption is labeled
- TAM, SAM, and SOM are all shown and explained
