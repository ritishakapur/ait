# Capability-Ladder.md

## Purpose
How to map the progression of skills, certifications, or capabilities that stakeholders can climb.

## The Ladder Question
What's the progression from beginner to expert? What unlocks each level? What's the reward for climbing?

## How to Build It
1. Identify the tiers (like the Capability Framework: Simple → Functional → Judgment → Coordination → Higher order)
2. For each tier: what it means, what verbs are involved, what it looks like, examples
3. Identify the prerequisites for each level (what do you need to get there?)
4. Identify the rewards for each level (what do you get when you get there?)
5. Identify the gaps (what's missing between levels?)

## Example (VLE Certification Ladder)
| Level | What It Means | Prerequisites | Rewards | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Basic G2C services | CSC registration | 5-50 rupees per transaction | None |
| Level 2 | Financial services | IIBF exam (98 rupees) | 15-50% commission on insurance | Exam not promoted |
| Level 3 | Advisory services | RAP certification | 5000-15000 per month | Certification invisible to VLEs |
| Level 4 | Training others | Level 3 + experience | Mentorship income | No formalized pathway |

## Rules
- Make the ladder visible (don't hide it)
- Show the income jump at each level
- Show the cost/time to reach each level
- Flag where the ladder breaks (gaps between levels)

## What Good Looks Like
- Anyone can see where they are and where they're going
- The path from Level 1 to Level 2 is clear and actionable
- The income reward for climbing is quantified
- Gaps between levels are identified and flagged
