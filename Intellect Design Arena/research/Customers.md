## 3. Customer Decision-Makers

### Who Buys Banking Software?

| Role | Evaluation Criteria | Influence Level |
|---|---|---|
| **CIO/CTO** | Architecture fit, integration strategy, cloud readiness, security | High — technology evaluation |
| **Chief Digital Officer** | Customer experience, digital channels, omnichannel strategy | High — business requirements |
| **CEO/CFO** | TCO, ROI, vendor stability, implementation certainty | Very High — final approval |
| **Board-level committees** | Risk assessment, regulatory compliance, vendor track record | Very High — core banking migrations require board approval |
| **Business unit heads** (Retail, Wholesale, Treasury) | Functional requirements, product features | Medium — input to CIO/CDO |
| **Regulatory compliance officers** | Compliance fit, audit readiness, data sovereignty | High — regulatory approval required |
| **Procurement** | Pricing, contract terms, SLA | Medium — negotiates terms |

### Purchase Journey (Typical)

```
1. Need Identification (1–3 months)
   └─ Trigger: Legacy system end-of-life, regulatory change, digital transformation initiative
   
2. RFP/RFI Issuance (1–2 months)
   └─ CIO/CTO leads; business unit heads provide requirements
   
3. Vendor Evaluation (2–4 months)
   └─ Demos, proof-of-concept, reference checks, design thinking workshops
   └─ Intellect's differentiator: Design Thinking workshops create deep customer bonds
   
4. Proposal & Negotiation (1–2 months)
   └─ Pricing, contract terms, implementation timeline, SLA
   
5. Board Approval (1–2 months)
   └─ Board-level committees approve core banking migrations (multi-million dollar decisions)
   
6. Implementation (12–36 months)
   └─ CMMI Level 5 process ensures delivery certainty
   └─ Intellect's differentiator: Implementation certainty and on-time delivery
   
7. Go-Live & Support (Ongoing)
   └─ Annual support/maintenance (15–20% of license fee)
   └─ 95%+ retention rate
```

**Total Sales Cycle:** 6–36 months (shorter for neo-banks, longer for G-SIBs)
