## 1. Executive Summary

Intellect Design Arena is a product-led enterprise banking software company with strong niche dominance (retail banking, transaction banking, Islamic banking, NBFCs) and aggressive expansion into North America and AI. The company shows healthy growth (50.8% YoY) and profitability (18.5% EBITDA margin), but the research folder has significant data quality issues that prevent confident investment-grade analysis.

**The single biggest finding:** The total revenue figure used throughout this folder (₹1,800–2,200 Cr) is inferred from profit margins, not derived from actual financial statements. Every downstream estimate compounds from this inference. The actual revenue requires the FY26 annual report.

**The second biggest finding:** IBS Intelligence Sales League Table data — used as primary evidence throughout — is self-published and has close ties to Intellect. Deal volume rankings are directional momentum signals, not independent analyst assessments.

**The third biggest finding:** Purple Fabric (AI platform) is treated as an established product in multiple files, but it was carved out as an independent LoB in June 2026 with no public revenue data, no customer case studies, and no technical documentation. All Purple Fabric figures are speculative.

## 4. Top 5 Strategic Risks

| Rank | Risk | Confidence | Impact | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Revenue inference error** | High | Critical | If actual revenue differs materially from ₹1,800–2,200 Cr, every downstream estimate is wrong. This is the foundational risk. |
| 2 | **IBSi circularity** | High | High | IBSi data is used as primary evidence throughout but is self-published. If IBSi rankings are inflated, Intellect's market position is weaker than portrayed. |
| 3 | **Services revenue trap** | Medium | High | If implementation services remain at 25% of revenue, margins lag pure-SaaS competitors (18.5% vs. 25–30%+). The path to reducing services is unclear. |
| 4 | **Purple Fabric failure** | Very Low | Medium-High | If Purple Fabric fails to gain traction, Intellect's AI strategy loses its flagship product. The entire "AI-first" positioning depends on this. |
| 5 | **Pricing discount dependency** | Low | Medium | The 70–85% discount vs. Temenos is a competitive wedge but limits revenue per deal. If Intellect can't raise prices, growth is volume-dependent. |

## 9. Social Media Scraping — Feasibility Assessment

**Attempted platforms:** X/Twitter (@IntellectDesign), YouTube, LinkedIn

**Results:**
- **X/Twitter:** @IntellectDesign resolves to a Bournemouth, UK design studio — NOT Intellect Design Arena (Indian fintech). Posts require authentication.
- **YouTube:** Search renders but no video results extractable via webfetch.
- **LinkedIn:** Returns 404 — URL structure mismatch + anti-scraping.

**Verdict:** Social media scraping is **not feasible with current tooling** (webfetch + no API access). Platforms require authentication, rate-limit, or block programmatic access.

**Recommendation for future projects:** Add social media analysis as a "should-have" in the research pipeline. Use paid tools (Brandwatch, Meltwater, Sprout Social) or manual review. Document the limitation in Company.md's Source Limitations section.
