# Final-Deck.md

## Purpose
Template for creating the final presentation deck from all research files.

## How to Use
1. After all research files are complete, use this template to create the final deck
2. Pull key findings from each research file
3. Structure the deck for a 30-minute executive presentation

## Deck Structure

### Slide 1: Title
- Company name
- Analysis date
- Team members

### Slide 2: Executive Summary
- One paragraph: what we found, what it means, what to do

### Slide 3: Company at a Glance
- Name, what it does, when it started, how big
- Key numbers (users, revenue, growth)

### Slide 4: What They Claim vs. What the Evidence Shows
- 2-3 key tensions from Company.md

### Slide 5: Industry Context
- Industry boundary, structure, trajectory
- Key benchmarks

### Slide 6: Competitive Landscape
- Positioning map
- Key competitors
- Moats and vulnerabilities

### Slide 7: Customer Segments
- Who buys, why they buy, how much they pay
- Key pain points

### Slide 8: Stakeholder Map
- Value chain
- Key tensions between layers

### Slide 9: Revenue Architecture
- Revenue buckets
- Waterfall (ranked by earning potential)

### Slide 10: Unit Economics
- The honest math
- The income math problem

### Slide 11: Product Landscape
- Product catalog
- Gaps and whitespace

### Slide 12: Technology Stack
- Foundational stacks
- Key gaps

### Slide 13: Strategic Recommendations
- Top 3 recommendations
- Priority, impact, timeline

### Slide 14: Next Steps
- What to do in the next 30 days
- What to do in the next 90 days
- What to do in the next 180 days

### Slide 15: Blind Spots & Assumptions
- What we don't know
- What we need to verify

## Deck Rules
- One idea per slide
- Numbers over prose
- Tables over paragraphs
- Actionable over descriptive
- 30 minutes max
